A Note from the Bench

It started as a small thing — a carpenter pencil that fit where torpedo levels couldn't, and didn't get lost. Then we noticed how many were ending up in the bin. So we made it again, this time entirely from plastic that had already done its work somewhere else.

Four small things, done well.

Spec sheet · 01–04

Feature · The Recycled Story

Built from what was already here.

We didn't set out to make a sustainable pencil. We set out to make a better one. The recycled plastic came later, when we realised the trade's most-thrown-away tool didn't have to be made from anything new.

Now every Chubby's pencil starts as something else — a milk jug, a yogurt tub, a takeaway lid — and ends as the most reliable thing in your pouch.

Our pencils are FSC-labelled because the graphite is wood-encased; the body itself contains zero virgin material.

Field Use · Who uses it

Carpenters love them. So does everyone else.

It was meant for masons. Then carpenters levelled stringers with it. Then tilers used it to mark ceramic. Then electricians installed boxes with one in their teeth. Then we stopped being surprised.

It turns out the smallest tool on the jobsite is also the most universal. Who knew.
