◐ The Recycled Story

One pencil, two lives lived.

Before it was a pencil, it was something else entirely. Here's how a yogurt tub turns into the most reliable tool in your pouch.

i.

Collected

Post-consumer plastic — bottles, tubs, lids — recovered from the recycling stream before it heads anywhere worse.

ii.

Re-formed

Cleaned, shredded, and moulded into a carpenter pencil body. No virgin plastic added. No trees felled.

iii.

On the job

Sharpened, used, lent, lost, found, and at the end of its working life — recyclable all over again.

◐ The Pencil

Four reasons it earns its spot on the belt.

↻

100% recycled plastic

Every body is moulded from material that already had one life. We don't make new plastic. We don't cut trees.

◉

Integrated magnet

Snaps to scaffold, joist hangers, steel framing. The pencil stays where you left it — even on the third storey.

✦

Real graphite, 3× the life

Not pressed lead. Marks cleaner on rough timber and lasts three times longer between sharpenings.

◢

Built for tight corners

Slim enough for soffit work where torpedo levels won't go. The original mason's pencil, since adopted by everyone else.

◐ On the Jobsite

One pencil. Every trade.

It was designed for masons. Now it's in every pouch on the site.

From soffits to switchplates.

Carpenters use it for finish work. Tilers level ceramic with it. Electricians install boxes, outlets and fixtures. Masons get into corners no torpedo level can reach.

It is, simply, the pencil that does it all.
